Callouts help content teams separate guidance from narration, especially when a page mixes setup steps, caveats, and product context.

<Callout title="General information" tone="info">
  Use info callouts for release notes, rollout context, or editor guidance that should stand out without feeling alarming.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Good for workflow reminders" tone="success">
  Success callouts work well for approval checklists, publishing confirmations, and “you are ready to ship” moments.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Use before destructive steps" tone="warning">
  Reserve warning callouts for migration steps, redirect changes, or edits that could break navigation if they are skipped.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Use for breaking changes" tone="danger">
  Save red callouts for removals, irreversible edits, or migrations that can take a docs section offline if the order is wrong.
</Callout>

## When to reach for one

- before a step that changes routing, slugs, or category structure
- after a checklist where you want to confirm the expected outcome
- beside embedded examples where readers need a little interpretation

## Keep the copy tight

The pattern works best when the title says why the note matters and the body keeps the action concrete. Two short sentences usually beat a long paragraph.
